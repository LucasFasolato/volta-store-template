# Design System Document: The Editorial Merchant

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**The Creative North Star: "The Digital Curator"**
This design system moves away from the "grid-of-boxes" ecommerce trope and toward a high-end, editorial experience. It treats every product listing like a curated gallery piece and every landing page like a premium lookbook. 

The aesthetic is defined by **intentional asymmetry** and **tonal depth**. By utilizing extreme whitespace and a "no-line" philosophy, we create an interface that feels like it was designed by a boutique agency, not generated by a template. We prioritize the mobile-first WhatsApp journey, ensuring the transition from browsing to chatting feels like a seamless white-glove service.

---

## 2. Colors & Surface Architecture

### The Palette
The color system uses a sophisticated "Slate & Slate-Blue" foundation to allow the brand colors to pop.
- **Primary (`#2d2d2d`):** Used for high-authority elements and core brand identity.
- **Secondary (`#306855`):** Reserved for "Action" states—specifically the WhatsApp conversion path.
- **Surface Tiers:** These are the lifeblood of the system, used to create depth without lines.

### The "No-Line" Rule
**Explicit Instruction:** Designers are prohibited from using 1px solid borders to define sections. 
Boundaries must be created through:
- **Tonal Shifts:** Placing a `surface-container-low` section against a `surface` background.
- **Whitespace:** Using the `16` (5.5rem) or `20` (7rem) spacing tokens to signal a change in context.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as physical layers of fine paper.
- **Base Layer:** `surface` (`#f8f9ff`).
- **Secondary Content Areas:** `surface-container-low`.
- **High-Interaction Cards:** `surface-container-lowest` (pure white) to "lift" the product off the page.

### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
To add "soul" to the minimalist base:
- **Floating Elements:** Use `surface-container-highest` with a 80% opacity and a `backdrop-blur` of 12px for sticky cart bars and mobile drawers.
- **CTA Depth:** Use a subtle linear gradient from `primary` to `primary_container` (150-degree angle) to give buttons a slight tactile curvature.

---

## 3. Typography: The Editorial Voice

We pair **Manrope** (Display/Headlines) for its geometric authority with **Inter** (Body/Labels) for its clinical legibility.

- **Display-LG (Manrope, 3.5rem):** Used for hero statements. Apply `-0.04em` letter spacing to create a "tight" editorial feel.
- **Headline-MD (Manrope, 1.75rem):** Use for section headers. Always pair with generous bottom margin (`spacing-8`).
- **Body-LG (Inter, 1rem):** The standard for product descriptions. Increase line-height to `1.6` for maximum breathability.
- **Label-MD (Inter, 0.75rem, All Caps):** Used for categories or "New Arrival" tags. Add `0.1em` letter spacing for a premium feel.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth

### The Layering Principle
Hierarchy is achieved by stacking. A product card (`surface-container-lowest`) sitting on a category background (`surface-container-low`) creates a natural, soft lift.

### Ambient Shadows
Shadows must never be "black." Use a tint of the `on-surface` color.
- **Token:** `shadow-ambient`
- **Values:** `0px 12px 32px rgba(11, 28, 48, 0.06)`
- **Usage:** Only for floating elements (drawers, sticky CTAs).

### The "Ghost Border" Fallback
If a border is required for accessibility (e.g., input fields), use the `outline-variant` token at **15% opacity**. Never use 100% opaque lines.

---

## 5. Components

### Buttons (The Conversion Engine)
- **Primary CTA:** Gradient of `primary` to `primary_container`. Roundedness `full`. This is the "Buy/Order" button.
- **WhatsApp Secondary:** `secondary` background with `on_secondary` text. Used for "Chat with an Expert."
- **Interaction:** On hover/active, scale the button to `0.98` to provide tactile feedback.

### Modular Product Cards
- **Structure:** No borders. Use `surface-container-lowest` as the card base. 
- **Image:** Apply `roundedness-md` (0.75rem). Use a 4:5 aspect ratio for a fashion-editorial look.
- **Spacing:** Use `spacing-3` for internal padding.

### Elegant Mobile Drawers
- **Style:** Glassmorphism (`surface_variant` at 90% opacity + blur).
- **Handle:** A small horizontal bar using `outline_variant` at the top to indicate "swipe to close."

### Sticky Cart Bar
- Positioned at the bottom of the viewport.
- Uses `surface_container_highest` with a blur.
- Content: Price on the left (`title-md`), "Order via WhatsApp" CTA on the right.

## 6. Do’s and Don’ts

### Do:
- **Do** use asymmetrical margins. For example, a headline might be indented further than the body text to create visual interest.
- **Do** use the `secondary` green sparingly—only for the final conversion step to WhatsApp.
- **Do** use "Surface Dim" for footers to create a grounded end to the page.

### Don't:
- **Don't** use dividers (`<hr>`). Use `spacing-12` or `spacing-16` to separate content blocks.
- **Don't** use pure black `#000000`. Use `on_surface` (`#0b1c30`) for a softer, more premium contrast.
- **Don't** use sharp corners. Every interaction point must use at least `roundedness-DEFAULT` (0.5rem).

### Accessibility Note:
While we utilize soft contrast for aesthetics, ensure all functional text (Body-LG and Labels) maintains a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 against their respective surface containers. Use the `on_surface` and `on_surface_variant` tokens strictly for this purpose.
